The audit · key findings

Where provassn.com is losing ground.

Providence has a century of history and products people search for every day. The current site keeps most of that hidden from Google and the AI assistants people now turn to, and it makes the visitors who do arrive work too hard. A crawl of its ~35–40 pages turned up the seven problems below.

1

No schema markup anywhere

Not one page carries structured data: no Organization, LocalBusiness, FinancialProduct, or FAQ markup. Google and the AI engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overviews) read that markup to answer questions. Without it, provassn.com doesn’t appear in AI answers, even where competitors do.

2

Bilingual content, handled inconsistently

Some pages mix Ukrainian into English; most service pages are English only. Roughly 300,000 Ukrainians have settled in PA and NJ since 2022, and no competitor is reaching them in their own language.

3

An FAQ page with no FAQ markup

There’s already a life-insurance FAQ page, but it lacks FAQPage markup. That markup is what produces the expandable Q&A results in Google and the answers ChatGPT and Perplexity quote. Adding it is quick.

4

Calculators people give up on

The premium and retirement calculators are static forms that give nothing back until you submit. The versions buyers expect now move a slider and show the number instantly. Done well, a calculator is one of the best ways to turn a visitor into a lead.

5

Slow, and technically untidy

Each page ships 20-plus empty comment blocks left by plugins, images aren’t compressed, and nothing is cached. Estimated mobile PageSpeed sits around 45–60, and Core Web Vitals are likely failing. Google uses both as ranking signals.

6

A broken sitemap and duplicate pages

The sitemap shortcode appears to render empty, and two separate privacy pages send Google a duplicate-content signal. Both waste the crawl budget Google spends on the site.

7

No language switch, no standing call to action

A Ukrainian speaker who lands on an English page has no way to switch, and no page keeps a “Get a quote” or “Speak with us in Ukrainian” button in view. What sets Providence apart is hidden instead of obvious.
